Learning from Failure

Failure is something that we as humans experience it. Its basic human tendency to fail at something. Failure is what helps us grow as a person by learning from our mistakes. One way i learned failure was through the Mars Climate Orbiter. NASA- a well reputated space center who would be expected to never make a mistake when it comes to space missions failed due to overlooking a simple tiny error. The error being conversion of units from pounds-force seconds to newton-seconds.

The company that NASA got their thrusters from did their calculations is pounds while NASA used newton-seconds. The reason being its normal for space missions to use newtion-seconds, NASA thought they had already done the conversion but that was wrong. This caused the space craft to go off course ever so slightly which falied the mission.

Personally this case made me reflect on my own habits. often i rush projects without proper checking or calculations which leads to mistakes. This case now reminds me to double check my work and make sure theres nothing wrong with it. Overall the mission taught me failure is not about mistakes but also about improvment.
